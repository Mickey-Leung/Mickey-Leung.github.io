# Correlation-Aware Attribution Framework (CAAF)

## Overview
The **Correlation-Aware Attribution Framework (CAAF)** is a machine-learning-based feature attribution method designed to identify **optimal sensor placements** (OSP) for efficient and robust inference in complex physical systems.  
It extends traditional feature attribution by incorporating a **correlation-aware clustering** step to mitigate redundancy and ensure information diversity among candidate sensors.

CAAF introduces a universal, data-driven approach for sensor optimization across a range of nonlinear, multi-scale dynamical systems, from structural health monitoring to turbulent flow sensing.

---

## Motivation
Feature attribution assigns importance scores to model inputs, revealing which measurements most strongly influence a prediction.  
However, when input data are highly correlated, as is common in real-world physical systems, naÃ¯ve attribution tends to over-select redundant sensors clustered in the same region.  
CAAF addresses this by first clustering correlated candidates and then performing attribution on cluster centers, ensuring each selected sensor contributes distinct information.

## Application: Airfoil Lift Prediction under Gusts
In gusty aerodynamic environments, accurate sensing of flow disturbances is essential for active control and gust mitigation.  
We applied CAAF to determine **optimal surface pressure sensor locations** on a NACA 0012 airfoil subjected to gusts generated by a cylinder placed upstream.

  This configuration achieved consistently lower lift prediction errors than conventional methods such as Pivoted QR, Bayesian experimental design, and uniform sensor spacing.
